## Post 1

**Date:** ~June 2026
**Topic:** External topical maps and LLM manipulation for local businesses

Can LLM-manipulating listicles and brand mentions help local businesses rank better? Yes.

I intentionally use the word "manipulating," not "optimizing." Because in this context, we are not only improving a page. We are artificially changing document statistics across the web to create a new consensus around a brand, service provider, and entity.

The method is based on what we call "external topical maps." Multiple domains create a connected document network. That network repeats, aligns, and reinforces the same attributes, values, claims, services, and entity relationships for a specific local business.

From an external publication, you can technically get 3 things:
- A mention
- A claim
- A link

In this case, the main focus is not only links. It is mentions and claims. Because mentions and claims help form consensus. And consensus helps search engines and LLMs understand who the brand is, what it does, where it operates, and why it should be trusted.

This law firm reached record leads in May, mainly due to stronger local rankings — with an estimated query value of more than $500,000 in the accident and personal injury space.

For location-agnostic queries, do not only look at national rankings. For a local law firm, an informational page is not successful only when it ranks broadly. It is successful when it strengthens local demand, local impressions, and local entity relevance.

That is how informational SEO supports local SEO. That is how external topical maps support brand consensus. And that is how mentions, claims, and document networks can influence both search engines and LLMs.

## Post 2

**Date:** ~June 2026
**Topic:** What is an Authority Signature — Google patent breakdown

Google has a concept called "Authority Signature," which appears in a patent that carries "Topic Authority" in its name.

The patent explains that an author's expertise, and the author's signature on a document associated with a specific topic, can increase the topical authority of that document. Google can evaluate not only the document and the website, but also the person-like entity behind the content.

The simplified formula for Authority Signature is:
Authority Signature Update = Authorship Percentage of Author A for Topic T in Document D × Topical Weight of Topic T in Document D

This means the system does not only evaluate whether an author exists. It can also evaluate:
- Which topic the author contributes to
- How much of the document belongs to that topic
- How much of that topical section the author actually authored
- How consistently the author contributes to the same topic across different documents

Topical Authority is not only about how much a website covers a topic. It is also about who covers the topic, how consistently they cover it, and whether the system can recognize the author as a real entity with a measurable topical signature.

## Post 3

**Date:** ~June 2026
**Topic:** Ranking #1 in the UK for hair transplant Turkey — Visual Semantics and Algorithmic Authorship

Ranking #1 in the UK for "hair transplant Turkey."

This page was designed with Visual Semantics and written according to Algorithmic Authorship rules.

When we create a page, we technically follow four steps:
1. Visualization
2. Verbalization
3. Commercialization
4. Contextualization

For the hair transplant industry, the most important attributes are clear: Before-after results. Cost. Procedure steps. Hair transplant types. Customer reviews. Doctor and expert validation.

These attributes should not exist separately. They should be connected, multiplied, and structured across the entire page experience. Because a page doesn't rank only because it has text. It ranks because its visual, verbal, commercial, and contextual signals support the same search intent from multiple angles.

There was also an entitization problem with this brand. Its authority was diluted across three different domains. I used the Knowledge Graph API to understand whether these domains were sharing ranking signals for the same brand. Then, we moved the two extra domains into the main domain to consolidate the ranking signals. After consolidation, the same documents started to perform better.
