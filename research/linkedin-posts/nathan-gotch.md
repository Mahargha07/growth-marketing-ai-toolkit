## Post 1

**Date:** ~May 2026
**Topic:** It's never been easier to win in search — effort beats AI slop

It's never been easier to win in search.

Just take a look at most SERPs and AI citation sets. They're polluted with AI slop with no differentiation whatsoever. All it takes is 10% more effort and you'll smoke most competitors.

A perfect example is the keyword "best SEO books." I've ranked for this keyword for years, and I haven't touched this content once.

The reason why is very simple: I purchased every single SEO book, sat down for hours, read all of them, and then wrote a review of those books.

One of the key differentiators right now is simply effort. Everyone is looking for the quickest way to do things, the most automated way to do things, and the things that require the least amount of effort.

The pendulum will swing back to the other end, where putting in more effort will help you stand out — especially in a sea of people who have no interest in putting in any extra effort.

## Post 2

**Date:** ~May 2026
**Topic:** Most AI SEO advice is overcomplicated — SEO is still the lead domino

Most of the advice about "AI SEO" is overcomplicated nonsense.

At the end of the day, the best way to get your brand visible in AI answers is still to do good SEO and focus on search everywhere optimization.

If your brand has strong visibility across search surfaces and core retrieval sources, you're far more likely to show up in AI answers. SEO is the lead domino.

But I don't just mean SEO on your website:
- You should also be thinking about SEO on YouTube
- You should be thinking about visibility across every important retrieval source
- And the number one retrieval source is still search

Your brand needs to be present on the sources these systems are actually pulling from.

There's no real secret here:
- Consistent technical SEO on your website
- Building topic authority on your website
- Extending that authority across your other owned assets
- Earning visibility off-site on the sources that AI systems actually trust and retrieve from

## Post 3

**Date:** ~March 2026
**Topic:** 5 AI SEO skills worth learning in 2026

5 AI SEO skills that are worth learning:

1. **Prove value** — Track 4 metrics for AI answers: Is your brand mentioned? What position? How many platforms? Are your brand mentions on the sources of retrieval? This is a leading metric because it influences AI answers.

2. **Control the narrative** — Start with your homebase (your website) to feed AI correct inputs about your brand when direct retrieval is used. Most businesses are plagued with incorrect information spread across the internet. Use deep research from any AI to find inconsistencies.

3. **Build a knowledge base** — This is the single best way to create on-brand content if you're using AI. Use ChatGPT Projects for a simple solution or Client Brain in Rankability for something more powerful.

4. **Master link building 2.0** — Backlinks still matter because authoritative websites rank well in traditional search and search is a top retrieval source for AI. But AI platforms don't care about followed, nofollow, or even sponsored links. What matters is that your brand is mentioned on the top sources of retrieval.

5. **Use AI for SEO and coding** — We are shipping SEO changes 100x faster than ever before and getting results faster and with less effort. No human can compete when AI-driven SEO systems are dialed in. Layer on coding capabilities and it's a wrap.
