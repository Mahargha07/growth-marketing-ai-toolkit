## Post 1

**Date:** ~March 2026
**Topic:** The training data gap nobody is talking about

Very open to being challenged on this, but I am feeling like we're not talking enough about the training data gap.

If you are making content and allowing it to not ship, you could launch it a few days after a training cutoff and then for answers given by training data, you could be waiting 18+ months for the next model.

To me this is why if you are trying to really change something about your brand or your visibility, getting the quality content out there that can change training-based answers has a different ROI if it gets in before or after a training cutoff.

I'm also thinking a lot about the universe of factors that could influence what an AI says about your brand on a risk, impact, and trackability continuum.

Some tactics (listicles) are higher risk, fast impact, and high trackability — meaning they are the right match for a certain type of company.

Other tactics (publishing a study in an emerging space) are lower risk, slower, and mid level tracking.

But the CMO who is contemplating a soccer team sponsorship and asks me how it could impact AI answers is playing a different game. It's low risk, extremely slow, and very low on trackability — it's a long term play for 500 other reasons, of which AI is only one.

This is a complex time. Without a lot of easy answers. I love it.

## Post 2

**Date:** ~March 2026
**Topic:** GEO is bigger than where you rank in ChatGPT — especially for SaaS

Is the real moat for tech companies in being a default infrastructure recommendation in Claude or Codex?

I have built 5-6 vibe coded solutions. These tools are built on whatever the AI recommended when I was vibe coding. I didn't know what "Supabase" was 4 months ago. While vibe coding in Opus 4.5, it was recommended to help me build what I wanted, so I signed up and now I pay them $40/month. Same with Vercel.

My entire tech stack was recommended for me by Claude Code's training data, and I never questioned one of the recommendations.

This means getting into the training data is a MOAT because they just don't update the models training cutoff dates that often.

GEO could be a lot more than where do I "rank" in ChatGPT and AI Overviews, especially in SaaS and Infrastructure. A GEO firm could help you:

1. Optimize in training data as a typical default answer (every time someone asks about SMS, Twilio is always mentioned)
2. Find bad vibe coding "loops" where the major AI coding models don't give good recommendations — helping with retention

SaaS companies could lose customers not because of anything they did, but because the models haven't been nudged to know how to troubleshoot in certain cases.

## Post 3

**Date:** ~March 2026
**Topic:** How to determine what % of your job could be replaced by AI

I just got asked a very strategic SEO question by a client on domain consolidation.

Step 1 - Recorded my answer based on my experience
Step 2 - Pasted the client transcript into Claude Opus 4.6, Gemini 3 Pro, and ChatGPT 5.2 Thinking
Step 3 - Asked each to give a side by side comparison vs what I did — who "wins", what did I miss, what did you get?
Step 4 - Review the output, determine what % of my recommendations were already "in the machine"

Results:
- ChatGPT 5.2 = 35% overlap
- Gemini 3 Pro = 15% overlap
- Claude Opus = 20% overlap

Whether I like it or not, 15-35% of my work was in the machine.

Things you can do instead:
- Be cross divisional — none of these ever really mentioned paid search data
- Stay up to date — read practitioners like Aleyda Solis doing real SERP real estate tests
- Get your data normalized — combine data across clients so you can use an LLM to pull patterns

Use all the models as a collaborator. I wanted to show what that could actually look like.
