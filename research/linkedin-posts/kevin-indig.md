## Post 1

**Date:** ~June 2026
**Topic:** Why two brands with identical on-page SEO get cited by AI at completely different rates

Two brands can run identical on-page optimization and get cited by AI at completely different rates.

The gap is off-property reputation. AI reuses the trust already attached to the sources it pulls from, and it favors entities it already recognizes on a topic. It doesn't form a fresh opinion about your brand on every query.

Your owned blog feeds that, but it's one of the weaker inputs. The model has no reason to take your word for your own authority. The publications, analysts, and competitors that mention you give it something it can trust without asking you.

That's why I treat on-page work as just one lever for AI citations. With optimization roughly equal, the brand that wins is the one AI already trusts through other sources.

Before you tune another title tag, check which sources AI cites in your space and whether any of them mention you.

## Post 2

**Date:** ~June 2026
**Topic:** Minimal vs high reasoning in ChatGPT cite a completely different web

Minimal and high reasoning in ChatGPT cite a different web.

I ran 100 prompts twice through GPT-5.2 across 20 buyer journeys and 4 verticals, once with minimal reasoning and once with high. Data from Semrush's AI Visibility Toolkit.

3 findings:

1. Only 25.6% of cited domains overlap between the two modes. 99 of the 173 domains in high reasoning never appear under minimal.
2. Citation rate jumps from 50% to 68% with high reasoning. Avg sources per response nearly doubles (2.6 to 4.5). Fan-out queries go up 4.6x.
3. Brand persistence from Problem stage to Selection happens in 4 journeys under high reasoning. Zero under minimal.

Two implications for prompt tracking:
1. We need to split tracking by reasoning mode. An aggregate view averages 2 different systems.
2. TOFU content has value again. Brands cited early in a conversation tend to carry through to decision time, but only under high reasoning.

We don't know how many users keep reasoning on. The better proxy is query type, not plan tier. ChatGPT auto-routes hard prompts to Thinking mode regardless of paywall: multi-criteria comparisons, evaluation frameworks, compliance questions, complex builds.

## Post 3

**Date:** ~May 2026
**Topic:** Almost no cited sources are visible across several LLMs for the same prompt

Almost no cited sources are visible across several LLMs for the same prompt.

Across 2.6 million URLs split between commercial, informational, and other intent, the universal overlap rate stays roughly flat. Each LLM picks different sources most of the time, even where the query should constrain the answer set.

The retrieval logic — what each engine trusts and what format it favors — does more work than the query type.

That mutes a common AEO assumption: that bottom-of-funnel visibility converges across engines. Commercial visibility has the same fragmentation problem as informational visibility.

The best-of guide that wins in Perplexity probably isn't the same guide winning in ChatGPT or Google AI Overviews. Optimizing for one LLM and treating that as universal coverage is a planning error.
