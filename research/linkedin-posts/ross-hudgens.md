## Post 1

**Date:** ~June 2026
**Topic:** B2B GEO study — X vs Y content drives 2x more AI search traffic

Our new B2B GEO study significantly changed how we prioritize content.

We tested every comparison content template against AI search traffic across 116 B2B sites. One type came back double the next-best option.

To our surprise, it wasn't "best X software" or "X alternatives" pages — it was actually the versus content format (X vs Y) that performed best.

We pulled GA4 data on 1,112 transactional pages and correlated each content type against AI search referrals from ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Gemini, Copilot, and Meta AI over 90 days.

Versus pages had a Spearman correlation of 0.65, or roughly 2x the next-best content type.

The reason makes structural sense in retrospect. When an LLM answers a comparison query, it cites pages that match the "X vs Y" intent. Best of lists and alternatives pages compete with G2, Capterra, and affiliate roundups for the same citation slot. Direct comparisons have less competition and are a tighter intent match.

A few more findings:
- The first 20 transactional comparison pages created are where the traffic curve bends most. Going from 1-5 pages to 6-20 corresponds to a 350% median lift in AI search sessions
- Content explains about 28% of the variance in B2B AI search traffic. The other 72% is brand pull and category fit
- One in five of the top-traffic sites had fewer than five transactional pages — they got there through brand

## Post 2

**Date:** ~June 2026
**Topic:** Voice is one of the biggest AI unlocks for content production

One of the biggest AI unlocks for content is voice.

Voice recognition technology is getting better and is embedded in every major LLM, such that we can record a voice memo and get polished content back.

This means we use AI not to generate content from an existing corpus of repetitive information, but rather use our own insights via voice and have LLMs distill them into a polished message for public consumption. Combine this with a Claude skill that documents your preferred tone, and you've got an amazing 1-2 punch for quality and speed.

Data from Wisper Flow shows that most people can speak 3-4x faster than they type. Additionally, you simply can't type in some contexts — such as in the car or on a walk or run, some of the moments most likely to spark creativity.

One of the things I've found lacking about having AI ingest insights from different areas like email, transcripts, Slack etc is that it does so in fragments. Voice is direct depth, and therefore less likely to have missed the mark or be lacking nuance.

By giving an LLM a long form, 3-5 minute memo that is then cleaned up in your company's voice, that's highly likely to be outputted at a high level of signal.

## Post 3

**Date:** ~June 2026
**Topic:** Every brand with an SEO content strategy should run a content audit in Q2

Every brand with a SEO-focused content strategy should be running a content audit in Q2.

The game changed in the last six months. AI Overviews, LLM traffic shifts, and the run of Google updates have hit a huge swath of sites. What was performing in October isn't what's performing now.

Most teams won't adjust because of sunk cost fallacy. "We spent $40,000 writing these 80 posts, so we can't just cut them." Yes, you can. The spend is already gone. The only question left is whether keeping those pages up is helping or hurting the rest of the site right now.

Unperforming content isn't neutral. It pulls crawl budget from pages that could rank. It dilutes topical authority. It drags on engagement signals for pages that would otherwise concentrate equity.

Cut or rebuild for the current landscape and the pages you care about will have more room to breathe.

The teams winning the back half of this year are running this audit now, while competitors are still defending old assets. Judicious pruning compounds. Every quarter you wait, the gap will widen.
