## Post 1

**Date:** ~June 2026
**Topic:** AI is worthless without taste — the 4D framework

After 100s of hours with AI, I'm realizing Claude, ChatGPT, Perplexity... they're all worthless until you do these 4 things:

You know the ability to pick between three headlines and know which one will land? How to sense when your marketing is technically correct but nobody's going to care? It's called TASTE.

Good news: taste is something you can learn (and get good at).

I call it the 4D framework and it's honestly the answer to the whole "AI is coming for your job" panic.

1. Decide what you actually want to say.
2. Duplicate the people already saying it well. Blindly. Shamelessly.
3. Decode the rules underneath why it works.
4. Defy the rules

Whether you're studying Apple or Dr. Dre, it's the same pattern. People decided what they wanted to say, studied what was already out there, figured out why it worked and then decided what rules to break.

AI can match patterns. It can't decide which pattern to break. That's where YOU live, my friend. That's where the value lives.

If you're spending all your time building workflows to remove yourself from the process, you might be automating the wrong part.

And when everyone's letting AI do the thinking, we're gonna be the ones making stuff worth caring about.

PS - I could automate my client "3S" interviews. I like doing them. I catch things. Ideas hit me mid-conversation. Not everything that can be automated should be.

## Post 2

**Date:** ~June 2026
**Topic:** Lazy AI content sends a message — using AI to create better not to care less

Every piece of lazy AI content says the same thing to your reader: your time is worth less than mine.

That's the implicit message. Every single freaking time.

You should take the time to consume this. But it's not worth my time to create it for you.

Low-quality content used to be rare because it was more expensive to produce. You had to pay a writer. You had to care at least a little bit. Now it feels free. 20 bucks a month. Unlimited LinkedIn posts. Endless blogs. So the floor completely fell out.

Things like 5 em dashes in a post are symptoms. The disease is entitlement to someone else's attention.

Because I'm starting to feel like the default output of every AI tool is: sound smart, say nothing, respect nobody's time.

If you're using AI to write, great. If you're using AI to avoid writing, your audience already knows.

⚡ PS - I even used AI to "write" this post. Every week, a Claude skill integrated with Fathom's MCP pulls all my calls from the last 7 days. It analyzes them using Erica Schneider's MP4 process and reminds me of all the smart stuff I said.

I'm using AI to create better. Not to care less.

## Post 3

**Date:** ~May 2026
**Topic:** There's no such thing as "an AEO strategy" — there are 4

There's no such thing as "an AEO strategy." There are 4... and you're probably running the wrong one.

Most AEO advice is written for Series C companies with brand recognition and research budgets.

Which one you run depends on two things: Are you established or new? And are you in a niche or broad category?

1. **Established brand + niche category (the trap)**
You already rank for the obvious stuff. The temptation is to keep churning out the same playbook but niche means not enough searches. Avoid: trend chasing, "updated for 2026" rewrites, generic listicles.

2. **Established brand + broad category (the opportunity)**
You have authority, budget, and a broad category to dominate. Original research is the highest-leverage move on earth here. Do: industry benchmarks, signature frameworks.

3. **New brand + niche category (the grind)**
No brand authority, no budget for original research. Win by being so specific and so honest that the few people who find you trust you immediately. Do: founder daily logs, honest post-mortems, hyper-specific use case breakdowns.

4. **New brand + broad category (the fight)**
Broad category = tons of competition. New company = no authority. You can't out-content volume the giants. Be the most useful answer for high-intent BOFU queries. Do: case studies with real numbers, comparison pages that recommend competitors when right, switcher stories.

Most companies aren't cleanly in one quadrant - they're hybrids. Find your mix.
